Agent Practice · Module 07 · ⏱ 90 min

Marketing Agreements, Endorsements, and Athlete Branding

Brand-licensing structure. Morals and reverse-morals. Equity components.

Open library My account

Module overview

This module is about the deals that make athletes wealthy beyond their team contracts. By the end of this module you will be able to read, negotiate, and counsel on endorsement and sponsorship agreements; understand morals and reverse morals clauses; structure athlete branding for long-term commercial value; and recognize the right-of-publicity and Lanham Act issues that condition the field.

Learning objectives

  1. Identify the standard structure of an endorsement deal.
  2. Negotiate the principal deal points: grant of rights, exclusivity, term, deliverables, compensation, approval rights, morals clauses, termination.
  3. Recognize the brand-protection architecture: trademark, right of publicity, Lanham Act false endorsement.
  4. Structure athlete brand for long-term value, including IP registration, equity components, and post-career planning.

Q12 · Short answer
Two or three sentences — what is the practical reason an agent should negotiate aggressively over the scope of "exclusivity" in an endorsement deal?
Model answer: Broad exclusivity restricts the athlete from pursuing other endorsements in adjacent categories, foreclosing significant commercial opportunities. Narrowly scoped exclusivity (specific product categories rather than broad industry categories) preserves the athlete's ability to layer multiple deals across adjacent categories, often producing substantially higher aggregate compensation than one broad-exclusivity deal.

Practical exercise

The instructor provides a sponsor-favorable first draft of an endorsement agreement (drawn from Volume III, Form A-1 with hostile defaults). The student redlines from the athlete side. The redline must address: grant of rights (narrow), exclusivity (narrow), term (with athlete-favorable renewal mechanics), deliverables (capped), compensation (escalators, equity if relevant, charitable contribution), morals clause (tightened triggers, cure rights), reverse morals clause (added), approval rights (preserved with deemed-approval default), termination (with kill fee).

Rubric (20 points): Each area scored 2 points for the redline edit and 1 point for the rationale.

Discussion prompt

The morals clause has been used to terminate endorsement deals in connection with social-media posts, political activity, and minor legal issues. Where should the line be drawn between sponsor brand-protection and athlete free expression? How should the agent counsel a marquee athlete on accepting morals-clause exposure as a condition of a major deal?

Supplemental reading

"The morals clause is the brand-protection device of the modern endorsement deal." — Volume I, § 7.7.
