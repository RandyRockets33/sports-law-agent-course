Antitrust and the Nonstatutory Labor Exemption

Federal Baseball through Alston. American Needle. Brown v. Pro Football.

Open library My account

Module overview

This module explains why league rules — drafts, salary caps, free-agency restrictions, eligibility rules — survive in a country with antitrust laws. By the end of this module you will understand the Sherman Act framework as applied to sports, the baseball exemption, the nonstatutory labor exemption, the single-entity question after American Needle, and the Alston rule-of-reason standard.

Learning objectives

  1. Identify the Sherman Act § 1 framework as applied to league rules.
  2. Explain the baseball antitrust exemption and the Curt Flood Act limitation.
  3. Apply the nonstatutory labor exemption (the Mackey test and Brown v. Pro Football).
  4. Apply American Needle to single-entity questions.
  5. Apply Alston to compensation rules.

Q12 · Short answer
In two to three sentences, explain why the nonstatutory labor exemption is essential to understanding why the NFL Draft and the NFL salary cap survive antitrust attack.
Model answer: The NFL Draft and the salary cap are agreements among separately owned teams that restrain the labor market for players, and absent some protection they would be vulnerable to Sherman Act § 1 challenge as horizontal restraints. The nonstatutory labor exemption protects them because they emerged from collective bargaining with the NFLPA (a mandatory subject of bargaining, primarily affecting only parties to the CBA, product of bona fide arm's-length negotiation). Without the exemption, both could be struck down as antitrust violations.

Practical exercise

A league announces a new "competitive balance" rule that imposes a soft cap on individual player salaries (no individual player may earn more than 25% of the cap). The rule is unilaterally announced by the league office without consultation with the players union, and the CBA does not address individual player maximum salaries. Analyze: - Sherman Act § 1 applicability. - Single-entity defense after American Needle. - Nonstatutory labor exemption availability. - Likely outcome if challenged in court by a player and the union.

Rubric (15 points): Sherman Act framework (4), Single-entity analysis (3), Nonstatutory labor exemption analysis (5), Likely outcome (3).

Discussion prompt

If a players union were to disclaim representation tomorrow and the league's labor-market restraints were exposed to antitrust attack, which restraints do you think would most likely survive rule-of-reason scrutiny and which would not? Why?

Supplemental reading

"The structural takeaway: the league's labor-market rules survive because the union has bargained over them." — Volume I, § 13.4.
