The Sources of Law That Govern the Agent's World

Federal statutes, state statutes, private governance, arbitral jurisprudence, soft law.

Open library My account

Module overview

The sources of law that touch the agent's work are unusually varied. This module gives you a working knowledge of the federal statutory, state statutory, constitutional, private-governance, and administrative sources that recur in agent practice. By the end of this module you will be able to identify, for any given representation question, which source of law is likely to apply.

Learning objectives

  1. Identify the principal federal statutes that condition sports law (Sherman Act, Clayton Act, Sports Broadcasting Act, Curt Flood Act, NLRA, Title IX, Lanham Act, Ted Stevens Act, SPARTA, federal copyright, federal gambling statutes).
  2. Identify the principal state statutes (athlete-agent statutes, NIL statutes, right-of-publicity statutes, gambling statutes, workers' compensation, jock tax).
  3. Identify the private-governance instruments as quasi-law: league CBAs, league constitutions and bylaws, NCAA Manual, conference bylaws, international federation rules.
  4. Identify the arbitral and administrative sources: grievance arbitration awards, CAS jurisprudence, USADA arbitrations, NCAA infractions decisions, SafeSport adjudications.

Q10 · Short answer
In two or three sentences, why does an agent need to know the difference between the federal SPARTA statute and a state athlete-agent statute?
Model answer: SPARTA provides a federal floor of conduct standards enforceable by the FTC and state AGs; state athlete-agent statutes are the principal day-to-day registration and conduct framework that the agent must comply with in each state where the agent recruits or works. Both apply; the state statute is typically the more specific compliance burden.

Practical exercise

Take three current sports-law news items (assigned by instructor or chosen by student). For each one, identify which body of law (or combination of bodies of law) governs and what the agent's principal compliance or analytical obligations would be.

Rubric (10 points): Identification of sources (4), Analysis of agent's obligations (4), Synthesis across the three (2).

Discussion prompt

Soft-law sources — trade press, industry practice, player-association policy memoranda — often move faster than statutes and cases. How does the agent develop and maintain awareness of soft-law sources? What subscriptions, conferences, or relationships would you build into your practice?
