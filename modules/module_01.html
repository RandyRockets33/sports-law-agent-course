What Sports Law Is and How Agents Fit Into It

Define the field. Map the recurring actors and instruments. Place the agent in the system.

Open library My account

Module overview

Welcome to Sports Law for the Aspiring Agent. This first module establishes what sports law actually is, how it differs from other practice areas, and where the agent fits into the field. By the end of this module you will be able to describe sports law as a sector of practice (not a single body of doctrine), identify the principal actors in the field, name the principal instruments, and articulate the agent's distinctive position within the system.

This is the foundation module. Every later module assumes the framing established here.

Learning objectives

  1. Define sports law as a hybrid sector of practice that integrates contract, antitrust, labor, IP, agency, tort, constitutional, administrative, tax, immigration, criminal, and gambling law.
  2. Identify the cast of recurring actors: athlete, agent, team, league, players association, conference, institution, sanctioning body, sponsor, broadcaster, gambling operator, integrity monitor, investor, public entity, technology actor.
  3. Identify the recurring instruments: standard player contract, CBA, league constitution and bylaws, conference handbook, NCAA Manual, endorsement agreement, media rights agreement, venue agreement, athlete representation agreement, participation agreement, integrity policy, grant of rights, transaction documents.
  4. Describe the agent's position within the system and the agent's distinctive fiduciary obligations.

Reading assignment

  • Volume I, Chapter 1 — The Field of Sports Law (full chapter)
  • Volume I, § 2.1 — The four major North American professional leagues (overview only)
  • Practitioner's Guide, Chapter 1 — Understanding Athletes

Practical exercise

Take a current high-profile athlete contract negotiation in the news (the instructor may assign one, or the student may choose). Write a one-page memo that identifies:

  1. Each actor in the matter (athlete, agent, team, league, players association, sponsor, broadcaster if relevant, public entity if relevant).
  2. The principal instruments in play (the CBA, the standard player contract, the addendum being negotiated, the endorsement deals layered on, the league rules being relied on).
  3. The agent's role and the agent's fiduciary tension points (where does the agent's interest diverge from, or align with, the athlete's? Where does the agent's interest diverge from, or align with, the agent's own agency or law firm's?).

Rubric (10 points):

  • Identification of actors: 3 points (must capture athlete, agent, team, league, plus any others relevant to the specific matter).
  • Identification of instruments: 3 points.
  • Analysis of agent's role: 2 points.
  • Analysis of fiduciary tensions: 2 points.

Discussion prompt

The course identifies the agent as fiduciary to the athlete and only to the athlete. In practice, agents often work at agencies that represent dozens of athletes, including multiple athletes on the same team or at the same position. How does the agent maintain the single-client fiduciary loyalty when the agency has many clients? What structures can the agent put in place to preserve that loyalty in practice?

Supplemental reading

"The first thing the new athlete-side lawyer has to absorb is that the client across the desk is not a typical legal client. The athlete is usually young. The athlete has often been the most talented person in every room since age twelve. The athlete has been managed, scheduled, coached, scouted, recruited, advised, and praised by adults whose livelihoods depend on the athlete continuing to perform." — Representing the Modern Athlete, § 1.1.
