Labor Law, CBAs, Grievance Arbitration, and Discipline

NLRA framework. Impasse and implementation. Steelworkers Trilogy.

Open library My account

Module overview

This module is about how unionized professional sports actually work as a labor relationship. By the end of this module you will know the NLRA framework as applied to sport, the mandatory-permissive-illegal trichotomy, the duty of fair representation, the grievance arbitration jurisprudence, and the commissioner-discipline architecture.

Learning objectives

  1. Identify the NLRA framework as applied to professional sport.
  2. Distinguish mandatory, permissive, and illegal subjects of bargaining.
  3. Apply the duty of fair representation.
  4. Identify the grievance arbitration architecture in each league.
  5. Apply the Steelworkers Trilogy judicial-review standard to commissioner-discipline awards.

Q12 · Short answer
In two to three sentences, explain the strategic significance of "impasse" in CBA negotiations.
Model answer: Impasse marks the point at which the parties have bargained in good faith to a stalemate and the employer may unilaterally implement its last best offer. The strategic significance is that pre-impasse unilateral implementation is an unfair labor practice (*Katz*), but post-impasse implementation is permitted and retains nonstatutory labor exemption protection from antitrust attack (*Brown*). The timing and characterization of impasse therefore drives both labor-law strategy and antitrust exposure.

Practical exercise

You represent an NFL player who has been fined $50,000 by the team for missing two preseason workouts. The player asserts the absences were excused by the head coach and the team is unfairly penalizing him in retaliation for prior public statements. Map the grievance pathway: which grievance category (Injury, Non-Injury, Commissioner Discipline)? Who decides? What deadlines apply? What is the appeal pathway? What evidence will you marshal?

Rubric (15 points): Grievance categorization (3), Decider identification (3), Timeline (3), Appeal pathway (3), Evidence strategy (3).

Discussion prompt

The commissioner-discipline architecture in the NFL gives the commissioner broad authority to investigate, prosecute, and adjudicate player conduct. The judicial review standard is highly deferential. Some scholars and athlete advocates argue this concentration of authority is structurally problematic. What reforms (negotiated in the CBA, not imposed by court) would you propose, and what would the league reasonably resist?

Supplemental reading

"Awards under § 301 of the LMRA are subject to limited judicial review under the Steelworkers Trilogy. The arbitrator's award will be enforced if it 'draws its essence' from the CBA." — Volume I, § 17.2.
