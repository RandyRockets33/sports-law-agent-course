Crisis Representation and League Discipline

Commissioner's authority. Multi-track crisis response. Privilege architecture.

Open library My account

Module overview

This module addresses what to do when things go wrong. By the end of this module you will know the architecture of league discipline, the commissioner's authority and its judicial-review limits, the multi-track crisis-response framework (criminal, civil, league, sponsor, media), the privilege architecture for crisis representation, and the recovery and reconstruction phase.

Learning objectives

  1. Identify the sources of league disciplinary authority.
  2. Apply the Steelworkers Trilogy judicial-review standard to commissioner-discipline awards.
  3. Build the multi-track crisis-response framework.
  4. Preserve attorney-client and work-product privilege during crisis representation.
  5. Structure the recovery and brand-reconstruction phase.

Q12 · Short answer
Two to three sentences — why does the agent's privilege architecture matter during a crisis?
Model answer: During a crisis the lawyer, the athlete, and multiple advisors (PR, investigators, mental-health, family) must communicate intensively. Communications between the lawyer and the athlete are privileged; communications including third parties may not be unless structured through a *Kovel*-style engagement. The privilege architecture determines what can be discovered in subsequent litigation and what can be used by adverse parties, so it must be put in place before the crisis hits or at the earliest moment of crisis recognition.

Practical exercise

You receive a call at 7:00 a.m. from a marquee NFL client. The client's home was raided overnight by federal investigators on a financial-fraud matter unrelated to football. Local media has picked up the story and the team has issued a brief statement.

Build a 24-hour response plan covering: - Engagement of specialist criminal-defense counsel. - Communication protocol with the team. - Communication protocol with sponsors (which contracts trigger morals-clause notifications). - Communication protocol with media (initial statement, future statements). - Privilege architecture for the response team. - Family-and-entourage coordination. - Litigation hold for documents.

Rubric (25 points): Each element scored 3 points; integration of the plan scored 4 points.

Discussion prompt

Concentrating discipline authority in the commissioner has been criticized as structurally problematic — the commissioner serves as both prosecutor and arbiter. The NFL's CBA has gradually carved out some procedural protections but the basic architecture persists. If you were negotiating the next NFL CBA, what discipline-architecture reforms would you propose, and what would the league reasonably resist?
