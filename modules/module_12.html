Agent Practice in the New College Economy

NIL deal review. Collective relationships. Multi-state compliance.

Open library My account

Module overview

This module is the practical application module for the post-Alston / post-House college economy. By the end of this module you will know how to advise college athletes on NIL deals, how to coordinate with NIL collectives, how to handle the institutional and conference compliance interface, how to structure the agent-collective-institution relationship, and how to handle the international-athlete F-1 visa overlay.

Learning objectives

  1. Structure the agent-college-athlete representation appropriately under NCAA Article 12.
  2. Review NIL deals from a compliance, tax, and athlete-protection perspective.
  3. Interact with NIL collectives professionally and within institutional rules.
  4. Counsel on international-athlete F-1 visa considerations.
  5. Handle the transfer-portal dynamic from the agent perspective.

Q12 · Short answer
Two to three sentences — describe the agent's principal risk-management responsibility when reviewing an NIL deal from a collective for a prospective student-athlete who has not yet enrolled at the institution.
Model answer: The principal risk is that the deal will be characterized as an impermissible recruiting inducement rather than legitimate NIL compensation. The agent's responsibilities include: substantiating fair-market value for the services rendered, ensuring the compensation is not conditioned on the athlete's selection of the institution, documenting the actual services performed, and counseling the athlete and family on the enforcement risk (including the risk of NCAA action even if the deal is ultimately upheld).

Practical exercise

The student is given a fact pattern: a junior-year men's basketball player at a Power Four institution is negotiating four concurrent NIL deals — a national footwear brand, a regional restaurant chain, a podcast platform, and the institution's NIL collective. The athlete is contemplating entering the NBA draft next year but may return.

Write a one-page compliance and structure memo that addresses: (a) NCAA Article 12, 22 compliance for each deal; (b) institutional disclosure obligations; (c) fair-market-value substantiation strategy; (d) tax structure recommendation (loan-out entity?); (e) coordination among deals (category overlap and exclusivity issues); (f) draft-decision implications.

Rubric (20 points): Compliance per deal (5), Disclosure (3), FMV substantiation (3), Tax structure (3), Coordination (3), Draft implications (3).

Discussion prompt

You represent a high school senior with multiple state NIL law options because his college choices span California, Texas, Tennessee, Alabama, and Florida. Each state has a different NIL statute. How would you counsel the athlete and family on the legal implications of each state's law as a factor in the college decision? Where would you advise extreme caution?

Supplemental reading

"For the athlete-side lawyer, the practical guidance: NIL deals signed by prospective student-athletes are subject to heightened scrutiny." — Practitioner's Guide, § 7.5.
