Sports Law for the Aspiring Agent

Master Syllabus and Course Design Document

A Sports Marketing University Online Course

Course identity

  • Title: Sports Law for the Aspiring Agent
  • Subtitle: A practical certification course in the legal architecture every modern sports agent must master
  • Format: Fully asynchronous online; self-paced with recommended weekly cadence
  • Length: 15 modules (60–90 minutes of asynchronous content per module, plus 30–60 minutes of exercise time)
  • Total time: Approximately 25–30 hours of structured learning
  • Audience: People who want to become sports agents — including current college students, recent graduates, working professionals transitioning into agency work, and non-lawyer practitioners already operating in adjacent fields (marketing, scouting, financial services, sports administration) who need to formalize their legal foundation
  • Prerequisites: None. The course assumes no prior legal education.
  • Certification: Sports Marketing University Certificate of Completion in Sports Law for the Aspiring Agent (gated on mid-term + final passage of 70% and capstone submission)

Required texts

  • Primary: An Attorney's Guide to Sports Law and Practice, Volume I — Doctrine and Practice (provided to enrolled students as part of the course package)
  • Secondary: Representing the Modern Athlete: A Practitioner's Guide to Sports Law and Practice (provided)
  • Reference library: Full four-volume treatise (Vols. I–IV) provided as a digital reference set so students can consult full case text, full statutes/CBAs, and the full document bank during exercises

Course outcomes

By the end of the course, the certificate-holder will be able to:

  1. Explain the institutional structure of professional and college sports and identify the legal actors, instruments, and forums that govern each.
  2. Read player contracts, marketing agreements, NIL deals, and league CBAs as a working practitioner.
  3. Identify and apply the rules of agent certification, registration, and conduct (player-association certifications, state athlete-agent statutes, SPARTA, NCAA Article 12).
  4. Negotiate basic athlete-side and team-side contract points.
  5. Spot antitrust, labor, IP, agency, contract, and regulatory issues in concrete representation scenarios.
  6. Counsel athletes through the principal career-arc transitions (entry, first major contract, decline, retirement).
  7. Manage advisor teams, financial-advisor selection, and crisis events.
  8. Operate within ethical and fiduciary frameworks that apply to agents.
  9. Understand the post-Alston / post-House college-athlete economy, including NIL and revenue-sharing.
  10. Apply the gambling-integrity rules and recognize the conduct boundaries that protect the agent and the athlete.

Grading and assessment structure

  • Module quizzes (15): 30% of final grade. Auto-graded multiple choice + short answer. Two attempts allowed per quiz. Best score recorded.
  • Practical exercises (15): 30% of final grade. Submitted for review; graded against published rubric. Some are auto-checkable; others are instructor-reviewed (rubric provided).
  • Mid-term assessment: 15% of final grade. Cumulative quiz on Modules 1–7 + one drafting exercise.
  • Final assessment: 15% of final grade. Cumulative quiz on Modules 8–15 + one drafting exercise.
  • Capstone project: 10% of final grade. A multi-part client representation simulation.
  • Discussion-board participation: Recommended but ungraded.
  • Passing threshold for certificate: 70% weighted final grade.

Course architecture and module map

The course is built in four arcs. Each arc closes with a synthesizing element.

Arc 1 — Foundations (Modules 1–3). What the field is, how it is structured, what the sources of law are. The student emerges able to navigate the field.

Arc 2 — The Agent's Core Practice (Modules 4–8). Agent regulation; player contracts; marketing and endorsements; NIL; negotiation. The student emerges with the practical capabilities of an entry-level agent.

Arc 3 — Antitrust, Labor, College Sport, and the Modern Economy (Modules 9–12). The doctrinal frameworks that shape the agent's working environment. The student emerges able to operate in the post-Alston / post-House college economy and the unionized professional sports environment.

Arc 4 — Disputes, Integrity, Crisis, and Professional Responsibility (Modules 13–15). Discipline, gambling, ethics. The student emerges able to protect the athlete and the agent across the kinds of events that end careers.

Module-by-module map:

  1. What Sports Law Is and How Agents Fit Into It
  2. The Institutional Architecture: Leagues, Teams, Conferences, Unions, NGBs
  3. The Sources of Law That Govern the Agent's World
  4. Becoming an Agent: Certification, Registration, and the Regulatory Floor
  5. The Agent's Fiduciary Duties, Conflicts, and Ethics
  6. Player Contracts in the Four Major North American Leagues
  7. Marketing Agreements, Endorsements, and Athlete Branding
  8. Mid-Course Synthesis + Mid-Term Assessment
  9. Antitrust in Professional Sport and the Nonstatutory Labor Exemption
  10. Labor Law, CBAs, Grievance Arbitration, and Discipline
  11. College Sport After Alston and House: NIL, Revenue Sharing, and the New Economy
  12. Player-Agent Practice in the New College Economy
  13. Crisis Representation, League Discipline, and the Commissioner's Authority
  14. Sports Gambling, Integrity, and the Conduct Boundaries Every Agent Must Police
  15. Final Assessment + Capstone Project

Per-module structure

Every module follows the same internal architecture:

  1. Module overview — what this module covers and why the agent needs it (3–5 minutes of reading).
  2. Reading assignment — specific section ranges from Volume I and the Practitioner's Guide. Optional source-document references to Volumes II–IV.
  3. Recorded lecture — 8–15 minutes (instructor-recorded from a provided script).
  4. Knowledge-check quiz — 8–12 multiple choice + 2–3 short answer.
  5. Practical exercise — one of: contract redlining, negotiation drill, issue-spotting memo, client-counseling script, regulatory-filing draft.
  6. Discussion-board prompt — one open-ended question for cohort discussion.
  7. Optional supplemental — a short clip from the Practitioner's Guide or a current trade-press item.

Certificate and credentialing

Upon successful completion (70% weighted grade including capstone), the student receives the Sports Marketing University Certificate of Completion in Sports Law for the Aspiring Agent. The certificate is verifiable via a unique credential ID. Sports Marketing University may, at its option, file for CLE accreditation in selected jurisdictions for those modules that qualify; CLE accreditation is not the primary objective of this course because the audience is non-lawyer aspiring agents.

Instructor and facilitator notes

This course is designed to be deliverable by an instructor who is not a lawyer, provided the instructor has working knowledge of the agent business and is willing to be the "voice" of the recorded content. The materials are scripted at a level that an experienced agent or sports business professional can record without prior legal training. The grading of the practical exercises requires either (a) review by a lawyer or experienced practitioner using the rubric, or (b) self-grading with rubric for students who are completing the course for self-development without a graded transcript.

File inventory

The course package is delivered as the following files:

  • SYLLABUS.md / Syllabus.docx — this document.
  • modules/Module_01.md through modules/Module_15.md — module-by-module content.
  • assessments/Midterm.md and Final.md — formal assessments with answer keys.
  • assessments/Capstone.md — capstone project with rubric.
  • quiz_banks/Module_NN_quiz.json — auto-graded quiz content in platform-agnostic JSON.
  • docx/*.docx — Word versions of every module and assessment, for offline distribution.

Implementation roadmap (for Sports Marketing University Operations)

  1. Production. Record the 15 lecture videos (one per module). Instructor may be a single host or a rotating panel. Each script runs 8–15 minutes; production cost low.
  2. Platform import. Load the modules into the chosen LMS (Thinkific, Teachable, Kajabi, etc.). The JSON quiz banks map cleanly to the major LMS quiz tools.
  3. Discussion infrastructure. Stand up cohort discussion boards or use the LMS's built-in tools.
  4. Practical-exercise review. Either hire a part-time reviewer (lawyer or experienced agent) or operate as self-grading using the published rubrics.
  5. Certificate generation. Implement automated certificate issuance with unique credential ID.
  6. Launch cadence. Cohort-based (monthly) or evergreen (continuous enrollment).
  7. Iteration. Plan for an annual content refresh because NIL/CBA/regulatory landscape moves.
